Top Universities To Study In Russia

Some of the top universities where you can study include: -

Crimean Federal University

Crimean Federal University, formerly Crimea State Medical University, has a prestigious legacy from 1918. As one of Russia's most prominent medical universities, it holds recognition from esteemed organizations such as the WHO and MCI. Since 1931, when it became an autonomous institute known as Crimea Medical Institute, it has been dedicated to providing quality medical education.

Volgograd State Medical University

Founded in 1935 as Stalingrad Medical Institute, Volgograd State Medical University achieved the status of a University in 2003. Renowned for its international collaborations and commitment to medical research, the University attracts attention from government officials across different countries.

Far Eastern University

Far Eastern Federal University (FEFU) is a leading institution offering MBBS education to Russian and international students. Established in 1899 by Tsar Nicholas II, FEFU is the oldest and highest-ranking University in eastern Russia. Accredited and funded by the Russian Ministry of Education, FEFU is among Russia's top five MBBS universities, ranked second in the national university rankings.

Bashkir State Medical University

Bashkir State Medical University, founded in 1932, is a renowned medical institution in the Russian Federation, attracting students worldwide. With a low-cost MBBS program and a diverse student community, it offers comprehensive training and opportunities for medical professionals.

Kazan Federal University

With a history dating back to 1804 as Imperial Kazan University, Kazan Federal University is a distinguished institution in Russia. Having obtained federal status in 2010, it has established partnerships with more than 190 universities and research centers across 53 countries, reflecting its commitment to global collaboration and academic excellence.

Academic Session Of MBBS Courses In Russia

The duration of MBBS courses in Russian medical universities varies from 6+ years to 7+ years, depending on the university type. For example, English medium universities have a 6+ year course, including one year of internship.

In comparison, bilingual or full-time Russian medium universities have a 7+ year course, including one year of internship and one year for learning the Russian language. The academic session typically begins in September, with the MBBS program starting around October 1st (subject to variation).

Language

While some medical universities in Russia offer education in English, it is essential to note that most of the population speaks Russian. Therefore, students must learn the Russian language during their studies.

Weather

Russia experiences a highly continental climate characterized by warm to hot, dry summers and freezing winters, with temperatures reaching as low as -30°C. Heavy snowfall is common, and the winter season typically lasts from the end of October to mid-March in some years.
